Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) Prevention Standards

Our Commitment to Child Safety

KutumbTree is committed to the safety and protection of children. We adopt and comply with internationally recognized standards to prevent child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE).

This document outlines KutumbTree's adherence to child safety standards and our policies to protect minors from exploitation and abuse.

⚠️ Zero Tolerance Policy: KutumbTree has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation. Any content, behavior, or activity that violates child safety standards is immediately reported to relevant authorities.

1. Standards We Follow

International Standards Adopted:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)

    We adopt NCMEC's CyberTipline standards for reporting suspected child exploitation.

  • Internet Watch Foundation (IWF)

    We follow IWF's guidelines for identifying and reporting child sexual abuse material (CSAM).

  • Thorn: Digital Defenders of Children

    We implement Thorn's best practices for preventing child exploitation online.

  • WeProtect Global Alliance

    We adhere to WeProtect's standards for online child safety.

  • Google Play Store Child Safety Standards

    We comply with all Google Play Store requirements regarding child safety.

2. How KutumbTree Protects Children

2.1 Content Protection

  • No CSAM: KutumbTree prohibits any child sexual abuse material (CSAM). Any such content is immediately removed and reported.
  • No Adult Content: We prohibit explicit, adult, or inappropriate content involving minors.
  • No Grooming: We prohibit any content or behavior attempting to groom, exploit, or abuse children.
  • Content Moderation: All user-uploaded content is screened for potential violations.

2.2 Age Verification

  • Account Restrictions: Users under 13 cannot create accounts (COPPA compliance)
  • Parental Consent: Users aged 13-18 must have parental/guardian consent
  • Verification: We verify age through account information and device settings

2.3 Privacy Protection

  • Minimal Data Collection: We collect only necessary information about minors
  • No Sharing: We never share children's information with third parties without parental consent
  • Parental Controls: Parents can control what information is shared about their children
  • No Contact Sharing: We don't share children's contact information without explicit consent

2.4 User Verification

  • Email Verification: All accounts require email verification
  • Identity Verification: Family invitations require email confirmation
  • User Reports: Users can report suspicious accounts
  • Account Review: We review reported accounts for violations

3. Technical Safeguards

3.1 File Upload Restrictions

  • File Type Validation: Only image and document formats allowed
  • File Size Limits: Maximum 100MB per file to prevent abuse
  • Metadata Removal: EXIF data stripped from uploaded images
  • Virus Scanning: All uploads scanned for malware

3.2 Communication Restrictions

  • Limited Messaging: No direct messaging between strangers
  • Family Only: Communication restricted to invited family members
  • No Real-Time Chat: No instant messaging with non-family users
  • Message Logging: All communications logged for safety review

3.3 Encryption & Security

  • HTTPS Encryption: All data encrypted in transit
  • Database Security: User data encrypted at rest
  • Access Controls: Role-based access to sensitive data
  • Audit Logs: All data access logged and monitored

4. Reporting & Enforcement

4.1 How We Handle Reports

  1. Immediate Action: Reported content is immediately flagged for review
  2. Investigation: Our team investigates within 24 hours
  3. Removal: Violating content is removed immediately
  4. Account Action: Violating accounts are suspended or terminated
  5. Authority Reporting: Serious violations reported to law enforcement

4.2 Reporting Mechanisms

  • In-App Reporting: "Report" button on suspicious content
  • Email Report: safety@kutumbtree.com for serious concerns
  • CyberTipline: NCMEC CyberTipline for CSAM
  • Anonymous Reporting: Users can report anonymously
CSAM Reporting: If KutumbTree discovers Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM), we:
  1. Immediately remove the content
  2. Preserve evidence for authorities
  3. Report to NCMEC CyberTipline at cybertipline.org
  4. Report to law enforcement (FBI, local police)
  5. Cooperate fully with investigation

5. Parental Controls & Transparency

5.1 What Parents Can Do

  • Consent Required: Parents must authorize their child's account
  • Control Visibility: Parents can control what information about their child is visible
  • Manage Invitations: Parents can approve/reject family member invitations
  • Monitor Activity: Parents can view their child's family tree activity
  • Delete Account: Parents can request deletion of child's account at any time

5.2 Transparency

  • Clear Policy: Parents receive clear notification of our CSAE standards
  • Data Practices: We clearly explain what data we collect about children
  • Safety Features: We explain safety features and protections
  • Contact Info: Easy way to contact us with concerns

6. Staff Training & Accountability

6.1 Employee Training

  • CSAE Training: All staff trained on CSAE recognition and prevention
  • Reporting Procedures: Clear procedures for reporting violations
  • Regular Updates: Training updated annually with new standards
  • Third-Party Training: Contractors and vendors trained on CSAE standards

6.2 Accountability

  • Review Process: Quarterly review of child safety practices
  • Incident Log: We maintain log of reported violations and actions taken
  • Continuous Improvement: Policies updated based on lessons learned
  • External Audit: Annual independent audit of child safety practices

7. Prohibited Content & Behavior

7.1 Absolutely Prohibited

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)
  • Grooming or solicitation of minors
  • Sexual content involving minors
  • Exploitation or abuse of children
  • Attempts to obtain children's personal information
  • Bullying or harassment of minors
  • Exposure to adult content
  • Any form of child endangerment

7.2 Violations Result In:

  • Immediate account termination
  • Report to law enforcement
  • Permanent ban from the platform
  • Cooperation with investigations
  • Preservation of evidence

9. Reporting Child Safety Concerns

Report to KutumbTree:

  • Email: safety@kutumbtree.com
  • In-App: Use "Report" button on suspicious content
  • Response Time: We respond within 24 hours

Report to Authorities:

  • CSAM: NCMEC CyberTipline (cybertipline.org)
  • FBI Tip: FBI Tips (tips.fbi.gov)
  • Local Law Enforcement: Contact your local police department
  • International: Internet Watch Foundation (UK)
Important: If you know of a child being exploited, please report immediately to law enforcement and/or the CyberTipline. Do not delay.
